Gym Training, Progress, and Focus

I like training that is simple to follow and easy to track. The goal is consistent effort, measurable progress, and enough recovery to repeat strong sessions week after week.

Mike Mentzer Visualization

Mike Mentzer (HIT in One Page)

Mike Mentzer is closely associated with High-Intensity Training (HIT). The theme is efficiency: fewer hard sets, high focus, and recovery treated as part of the program. Even if you don’t follow HIT strictly, the mindset is useful: avoid “junk volume,” keep technique honest, and make your work sets count.

My approach borrows the best parts—intentional effort and recovery—while staying practical and sustainable.

Weekly Structure

Simple routines are easier to execute. Here’s an ordered plan with an unordered list inside.

  1. Strength (3 days)
    • Push: press + accessory work
    • Pull: row/pulldown + accessory work
    • Legs: squat/hinge patterns
  2. Conditioning (2 days)
    • Zone 2 steady state
    • Intervals (short, controlled)
  3. Daily habits
    • Mobility (5–10 min)
    • Sleep and hydration
