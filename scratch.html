Why I Train

I train for strength, confidence, and long-term health. I like keeping things simple: a clear plan, consistent effort, and measurable progress. Instead of doing random workouts, I track the basics—sets, reps, and rest—so every week I can answer one question: am I improving?

Mike Mentzer (Quick Overview)

Mike Mentzer is known for promoting high-intensity training (often called HIT): fewer sets, very high effort, and a strong emphasis on recovery. The main idea is straightforward—if a set is truly hard and close to failure, you may not need a huge number of sets to stimulate growth. That philosophy resonates with me because it forces you to train with purpose, not just volume for the sake of volume.

My approach is not “all or nothing.” I borrow the spirit of Mentzer’s message—effort, focus, recovery—while still keeping my training flexible and sustainable.

Weekly Structure (Nested List Requirement)

  1. Strength (3 days)
    • Push: bench or incline + overhead press + triceps
    • Pull: rows or pulldowns + rear delts + biceps
    • Legs: squat pattern + hinge pattern + calves
  2. Cardio (2 days)
    • Zone 2 steady state (20–40 minutes)
    • Intervals (short, controlled, not reckless)
  3. Mobility (daily)
    • Hips, shoulders, ankles (5–10 minutes)

I keep the plan simple so it’s easy to follow. Consistency beats complexity.
